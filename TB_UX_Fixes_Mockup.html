What's Broken in the Current Experience
Based on the video walkthrough and course screenshot

1. "Where Am I?" Is Unanswerable

The course shows "5 / 366 Activities" with no map, no numbering, no progress trail. A 7-year-old sees an infinite scroll of identically-named rows. They have no sense of beginning, middle, or end.

2. One Lesson Looks Like Three

Each lesson expands into Video, Article, Exercise as separate rows with identical names. Kids think "Comparison word problems" appears 3 times because it's 3 different things. It's actually one lesson with 3 parts.

3. No Way Back to Content

When a student gets stuck on practice Q8, there's no link to rewatch the video or reread the article. The only option is the tiny "Back" arrow which dumps them back to the course list. The learning content and practice are totally disconnected.

4. Videos Are Skippable, No Gate

Students can jump straight to the Exercise (which they will) and skip the video instruction entirely. When they fail, there's no mechanism to send them back to the video. They'll just guess repeatedly.

5. Non-Readers Get No Support

Articles are plain text with no TTS (text-to-speech). Many K-2 students can't read the instructional text. They'll see a wall of words and click past it. The learning content might as well not exist.

6. No Worked Examples on Failure

Zearn walks students through each step. When this TB exercise shows a wrong answer, it shows confetti for the right one but doesn't explain why. There's no worked example, no scaffolding, no "let me show you."

Implementation Summary
6 high-impact fixes, ranked by effort and impact

Fix 1: Collapse 3 sub-activities into 1 lesson row

EFFORT: Low (display logic only)  •  IMPACT: High

Each lesson shows as a single row in the course list. Internally it still has Video, Article, Exercise — but the student sees "Lesson 2: Comparison word problems" with a Watch/Learn/Practice step bar inside the lesson, not as separate course entries. This is purely a front-end grouping change.

Fix 2: Add numbered lesson path with "You Are Here"

EFFORT: Low-Medium (UI change)  •  IMPACT: High

Replace "5 / 366 Activities" with "Lesson 2 of 8" + a visual progress bar. Number every lesson. Show a pulsing "You are here" indicator on the current lesson. Group lessons into units. A child should be able to look at the screen and say "I'm on lesson 2, I have 6 more to go."

Fix 3: TTS on every text element

EFFORT: Medium (use Web Speech API or ElevenLabs)  •  IMPACT: Critical for K-2

Add a 🔈 button to every question, every article paragraph, every worked example. For questions, auto-read aloud on page load. The browser's built-in SpeechSynthesis API can do this with zero cost. For higher quality, use ElevenLabs or Google Cloud TTS.

Fix 4: Video completion gate before Practice

EFFORT: Low (conditional check)  •  IMPACT: High

Track video watch percentage. If a student clicks "Practice" before watching >80% of the video, show a friendly redirect: "Watch the video first!" with a button that takes them there. This prevents the skip-and-guess pattern.

Fix 5: Help buttons on every practice question

EFFORT: Medium (link content to questions)  •  IMPACT: High

Three buttons below every question: 💡 Show example (inline worked example), 🎥 Rewatch video (opens video overlay), 📖 Reread lesson (shows article inline). After 2 consecutive wrong answers, the worked example auto-opens. This is the biggest gap vs. Zearn.

Fix 6: Inline question progress dots

EFFORT: Low (UI addition)  •  IMPACT: Medium

Replace "Question 5 / EASY DIFFICULTY" with a visual dot trail showing green (correct), red (wrong), blue (current), gray (upcoming). The child sees their progress through the exercise at a glance — like IXL's SmartScore but simpler.

Printable Workbooks (Offline Fix)

For articles: print them as workbook pages — article content on the left, blank workspace on the right. Students can write, draw, and show their work physically. This bridges the gap between Zearn's step-by-step guided approach and TB's digital-only format. This doesn't require any platform changes — just a PDF export of each lesson's article content formatted for printing.
