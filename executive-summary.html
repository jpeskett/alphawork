Executive Summary: Why Students Didn't Achieve 2× Growth

Based on analysis of 398 math students with Fall-to-Winter growth data, this report identifies three systemic curriculum issues preventing students from achieving predicted growth rates.

Overall Finding: 227 students (57.2%) did not achieve 2× growth. Average actual growth was 2.08× versus 2.65× predicted—representing a significant 22% performance gap.

The Three Root Causes

1. The Grade 6 Number Systems "Black Hole"

The Problem: Math Academy Grade 5 focuses heavily on fraction/decimal procedures, while MA Prealgebra jumps straight to high school level radicals and exponents.

The Gap: Critical middle-tier skills are skipped entirely:

  • Integer operations (adding/subtracting negatives)
  • Absolute value concepts
  • Four-quadrant coordinate plane

Impact: Students attempt Algebra II content while missing the Grade 6 foundations that drive MAP RIT scores.

2. Procedural Over-Focus vs. Conceptual Mastery

The Problem: Curriculum prioritizes high-volume repetition of procedural tasks over conceptual understanding.

Evidence:

  • Rocco repeated Metric Units 15 times but failed "Interpreting Fraction Division" (conceptual) 3 times
  • Shepperd "completed" Prealgebra with only 40% quiz pass rate
  • Students stuck in "Negative XP" loops without remediation

Impact: Students "brute-force" through lessons without gaining true understanding.

3. Inverted Priority (Strength-Sapping Effect)

The Problem: Curriculum directs students to work on their strengths rather than their weaknesses.

Examples:

  • Bailey: 280 RIT in Geometry (strength), 260 in Number Systems (weakness) → received 126 Geometry activities, only 68 Number Systems
  • Rhys: Weakness in Algebra → forced through high-level Algebra until score regressed by 7 points

Impact: Students get better at what they're already good at while weaknesses persist.

Why This Happens: RIT 220-245 "Dead Zone"

Students in this mid-range RIT band face unique challenges:

  • Content gaps: Topics covered don't match what NWEA MAP tests at grades 4-5 (fractions, decimals, ratios, early algebra, geometry)
  • Pacing issues: Students move through MA content but don't get enough practice on MAP-relevant skills
  • Depth vs breadth mismatch: MA goes deep on certain topics while MAP tests breadth across grade-level standards
  • Ceiling effects: Not low enough for intensive intervention, not advanced enough for HS content benefits

Comprehensive Recommendations

1. Implement MAP-Informed Placement

Stop placing students based on chronological grade or generic curriculum labels.

Action: Sync MAP RIT scores to curriculum weekly. If a student's Number Systems RIT is <225, the system should lock advanced Algebra modules until specific Grade 6 "Bridge Topics" (integers, absolute value, coordinate plane) are mastered.

2. Create a "Bridge Module" for Grade 6

Build a mandatory 4-week intensive module covering:

  • Integer Fluency: All four operations with negative numbers
  • Ratios & Proportions: The missing middle school bridge content
  • Statistics & Probability: Moving beyond line plots to measures of center (mean/median/mode)
  • Coordinate Plane: All four quadrants with integer coordinates
  • Absolute Value: Conceptual understanding and applications

3. Shift from "Retake" to "Remediate"

Implement a "Circuit Breaker" Rule: After 3 failed attempts at a topic:

  • Forward progression stops automatically
  • System drops student back to a prerequisite skill one grade level lower
  • Student must pass 3 consecutive "High Success" lessons to rebuild confidence
  • Only then return to target topic

Why: Currently, students fail topics 10+ times without intervention. This creates "spinning wheels" phenomenon—high activity, zero learning.

4. Rebalance Activity Weighting

Force curriculum to prioritize lowest RIT domain:

  • Algorithm ensures 2:1 ratio of activities in Weakness vs. Strength domains
  • Continue until RIT scores are within 5-10 points of each other
  • Cap activities in strength areas once mastery demonstrated

5. Audit Grade 4-5 Content Against NWEA MAP

Immediate Actions:

  • Map MA Grade 4-5 content to NWEA MAP RIT bands 200-240
  • Identify coverage gaps in Number Systems and Statistics domains
  • Add MAP-aligned practice for students in 5th Grade and Prealgebra courses
  • Supplement Prealgebra students with targeted practice in: operations with fractions/decimals, ratios & proportions, expressions & equations

6. Address Alpha School Austin Specifically

With 131 students showing 1.25× average growth:

  • Conduct site visit to understand implementation differences
  • Review teacher training and support structures
  • Investigate if curriculum pacing or usage differs from other campuses
  • Provide targeted professional development
  • Consider pilot interventions at this campus first given large sample size

Conclusion

The data reveals this is not an effort problem—students are putting in the time (R90 grades show consistent usage). This is a curriculum alignment problem. The gap between what students are learning and what NWEA MAP tests is particularly severe in the RIT 220-245 range (Grades 4-6).

The good news: we know what works. SAT Math Fundamentals achieves 8.60× growth because it aligns with what's tested. We need to apply those same principles to middle school content.

Path Forward: By implementing MAP-informed placement, creating targeted bridge modules, establishing remediation loops, and rebalancing curriculum priorities, we can move the 227 students currently below 2× growth toward their predicted performance levels. The curriculum has the right content—it's the sequencing and emphasis that need adjustment.
